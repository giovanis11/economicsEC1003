# EC1003 - Economics of Everyday Life

## Practice Final Exam - Alternative Version

**Coverage:** GDP and economic growth; business cycle; unemployment and inflation; aggregate demand and aggregate supply; fiscal policy; money, interest rates and present value; monetary policy.

**Instructions:** Choose the one best answer for each question.

## Multiple Choice

1. Gross Domestic Product counts only final goods and services mainly because:

    A) intermediate goods are never sold in markets  
    B) it avoids counting the same production more than once  
    C) foreign firms do not keep accounting records  
    D) government purchases are excluded from GDP  

2. A German-owned company produces tires in Tennessee. Those tires are:

    A) counted in U.S. GDP because they are produced in the United States  
    B) counted in German GDP because the company is German-owned  
    C) excluded from every country's GDP  
    D) counted only if they are exported  

3. The expenditure approach to GDP is best written as:

    A) wages + rent + interest + profit  
    B) consumption + taxes + imports + savings  
    C) consumption + investment + government purchases + net exports  
    D) income + depreciation - exports  

4. A U.S. store imports a tablet for $300 and sells it to a customer for $420. The direct effect on U.S. GDP is:

    A) $0  
    B) $120  
    C) $300  
    D) $420  

5. A refrigerator is produced in December but sold to a household in January. In GDP accounting, it is:

    A) counted only when the customer buys it  
    B) never counted because it was stored as inventory  
    C) counted as government spending in December  
    D) counted as inventory investment when produced, with the sale adjusting inventory later  

6. A market basket costs $500 in the base year and $575 this year. The price index this year is:

    A) 75  
    B) 100  
    C) 115  
    D) 575  

7. If the CPI rises from 160 to 168, the inflation rate is:

    A) 2.5 percent  
    B) 5 percent  
    C) 8 percent  
    D) 68 percent  

8. Deflation occurs when:

    A) a few prices fall but the average price level rises  
    B) the average price level falls  
    C) the inflation rate is positive but smaller than before  
    D) wages rise more slowly than prices  

9. If actual inflation is higher than lenders and borrowers expected, the group that tends to gain is:

    A) lenders  
    B) borrowers  
    C) people paid fixed nominal incomes  
    D) holders of cash savings  

10. Suppose 92 million people are employed, 8 million are unemployed and looking for work, and 50 million are not in the labor force. The unemployment rate is:

    A) 5.3 percent  
    B) 8 percent  
    C) 13.8 percent  
    D) 40 percent  

11. A worker loses a factory job because the firm permanently replaces that job with automated equipment. This is best described as:

    A) frictional unemployment  
    B) structural unemployment  
    C) cyclical unemployment  
    D) seasonal unemployment  

12. In a country with 200 million adults, 124 million employed people and 6 million unemployed people, the labor force participation rate is:

    A) 3 percent  
    B) 62 percent  
    C) 65 percent  
    D) 97 percent  

13. A sudden crisis in consumer and business confidence will most likely:

    A) shift aggregate demand left  
    B) shift aggregate demand right  
    C) shift aggregate supply right  
    D) leave both curves unchanged  

14. An increase in personal income taxes will most directly:

    A) increase aggregate demand  
    B) decrease aggregate demand  
    C) increase aggregate supply  
    D) decrease aggregate supply only  

15. An increase in government purchases, holding other factors constant, will:

    A) shift aggregate demand right  
    B) shift aggregate demand left  
    C) shift aggregate supply right  
    D) shift aggregate supply left  

16. Higher interest rates tend to affect aggregate demand by:

    A) increasing investment and consumption spending  
    B) reducing interest-sensitive spending  
    C) lowering input costs for producers  
    D) raising aggregate supply  

17. A sharp increase in oil prices would most likely:

    A) shift aggregate supply right and lower prices  
    B) shift aggregate supply left and raise prices  
    C) shift aggregate demand right and raise output  
    D) shift aggregate demand left and lower input costs  

18. An economy-wide increase in worker productivity will most likely:

    A) increase aggregate supply  
    B) decrease aggregate supply  
    C) decrease aggregate demand  
    D) increase unemployment by definition  

19. Demand-pull inflation can be caused by:

    A) falling oil prices  
    B) lower government spending  
    C) rising aggregate demand  
    D) falling productivity  

20. Cost-push inflation is most likely to result from:

    A) a decrease in interest rates  
    B) an increase in input prices  
    C) a tax cut that raises consumption  
    D) a rise in consumer confidence  

21. A supply-side economist would most likely support:

    A) higher regulations on business firms  
    B) a higher minimum wage requirement  
    C) lower taxes on production and investment  
    D) employer benefit mandates that raise labor costs  

22. The long-run aggregate supply model assumes that:

    A) both input and output prices can adjust  
    B) input prices are fixed but output prices are flexible  
    C) output prices are fixed but input prices are flexible  
    D) both input and output prices are fixed  

23. If both personal income taxes and business taxes rise, the likely combined effect is:

    A) aggregate demand rises and aggregate supply rises  
    B) aggregate demand falls and aggregate supply falls  
    C) aggregate demand falls and aggregate supply rises  
    D) aggregate demand rises and aggregate supply falls  

24. When the general price level rises, which statement is the exception?

    A) The purchasing power of money savings increases  
    B) foreign buyers may buy less domestic output  
    C) imports may become more attractive  
    D) net exports may decrease  

25. A progressive income tax and unemployment benefits are examples of:

    A) automatic stabilizers  
    B) open market operations  
    C) monetary policy tools  
    D) exchange-rate policy  

26. A temporary tax cut passed by Congress to encourage household spending is:

    A) nondiscretionary fiscal policy  
    B) discretionary fiscal policy  
    C) monetary policy  
    D) a reserve requirement change  

27. Expansionary fiscal policy in the short run usually:

    A) shifts aggregate demand right  
    B) shifts aggregate demand left  
    C) decreases consumption by design  
    D) reduces government purchases  

28. If policymakers use fiscal policy to fight high demand-pull inflation, they would most likely:

    A) cut taxes and raise government spending  
    B) raise taxes or cut government spending  
    C) buy government securities  
    D) lower the reserve ratio  

29. Tax rebate checks sent to households to stimulate spending are an example of:

    A) fiscal policy  
    B) monetary policy  
    C) international trade policy

30. Automatic stabilizers help the economy because they:

    A) require a new law every time GDP changes

    A) politicians may time policy for electoral advantage

32. An unexpected increase in world oil prices is best classified as:

    A) a positive demand shock  
    B) a negative supply shock  
    C) a monetary shock only

33. An unexpected collapse in consumer confidence is best classified as:

    A) a negative demand shock  
    B) a positive supply shock  
    C) a reserve requirement change  
    D) an increase in productive capacity  

34. A surtax created specifically to slow inflation is:

    A) expansionary discretionary fiscal policy  
    B) contractionary discretionary fiscal policy  
    C) expansionary monetary policy  
    D) nondiscretionary monetary policy  

35. A new federally funded jobs program during a recession is:

    A) expansionary discretionary fiscal policy  
    B) contractionary discretionary fiscal policy

36. A leader who says the economy should rely on automatic stabilizers instead of new tax and spending laws is favoring:

    A) discretionary fiscal policy  
    B) nondiscretionary fiscal policy

37. Savers are mainly motivated by the:

    A) nominal interest rate only  
    B) real interest rate  
    C) exchange rate  
    D) unemployment rate  

38. In the market for loanable funds, the price is the:

    A) wage rate  
    B) inflation index  
    C) interest rate  
    D) exchange rate  

39. A higher interest rate tends to:

    A) encourage saving and discourage borrowing  
    B) discourage saving and encourage borrowing

    A) 3 percent  
    B) 4 percent

41. If the real interest rate is 2 percent and expected inflation is 5 percent, the approximate nominal interest rate is:

    A) -3 percent

    C) 5 percent  
    D) 7 percent

    A) 2,000 / (1.05)^3  
    B) 2,000 x (1.05)^3  
    C) 2,000 x (0.05)^3  
    D) 2,000 / 0.05

    A) 10,000 x (1.04)^5  
    B) 10,000 / (1.04)^5

    D) 10,000 / 5

    A) rises  
    B) falls  
    C) stays exactly the same  
    D) becomes equal to the future payment

    A) costs and benefits often occur at different times  
    B) it ignores interest rates  
    C) it counts only cash held by banks  
    D) it converts all investments into government spending

    A) discount window  
    B) reserve ratio  
    C) internal rate of return

    A) 6 years  
    B) 9 years

    A) corporate stocks
